Taiwan

Facts
  • It is the most populous state and largest economy that is not a member of the United Nations
  • Taiwan's government is a democratic republic with five branches: the executive, legislative, and judicial components plus a Control Yuan (branch of government) and an Examination Yuan.
  • The tallest building is Taiwan is Taipei 101, which measures at 1,671 ft or 509.2 meters
History
  • It was a colony of the Netherlands for about 40 years in the early to mid-17th century and was subsequently independent again for about two decades.
  • China gained control there in the late 17th century and ruled Taiwan for some two centuries. Japan acquired Taiwan in 1895 following the first Sino-Japanese War, and it became a colony.
  • Taiwan was returned to Nationalist Chinese control in 1945 following Japan's defeat in World War II. Today, there is controversy over its status as a country.

Germany

Facts
  • Escaping Prison is legal in Germany
  • Germany has a mueseum dedicated to snoring
  • Some of Germany's highways have no speed limit
History
  • Originally a Confederation of states known as the Holy Roman Empire, which was disbanded by Napoleon and turned into the German Confederation
  • Officially founded in 1871, when Prime Minister Otto Von Bismarck united the German confederation.
  • Fought in both World Wars, and was divided in East and West Germany during the Cold War until it was reunited in 1991
